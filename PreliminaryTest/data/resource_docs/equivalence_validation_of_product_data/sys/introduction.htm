Introduction

ISO 10303 is an International Standard for the computer-interpretable representation of product information and for the exchange of product data. The objective is to provide a neutral mechanism capable of describing products throughout their life cycle. This mechanism is suitable not only for neutral file exchange, but also as a basis for implementing and sharing product databases, and as a basis for retention and archiving.

Major subdivisions of this part of ISO 10303 are:

  • product_data_equivalence_definition_schema;
  • product_data_equivalence_criteria_schema;
  • product_data_equivalence_inspection_result_schema;
  • annotated_model_3d_equivalence_criteria_schema;
  • annotated_model_3d_equivalence_inspection_result_schema.

Before going into details of equivalence validation, it will be appropriate to discuss why equivalence validation of product data is required. All product model data are created by some CAD system and it is well known that created product data frequently travels two or more different IT systems to conduct expected applications in various types of design, simulation, manufacturing preparation, etc. For travelling from an IT system to other IT system, data conversion by the use of STEP translator, or other types of translators is required. As is well known, numerical representation method and satisfied precision are IT system dependent. Therefore, data conversion may cause slight difference of data. For users of the data, whether or not the difference of data caused by the conversion is acceptable for the applications expected to be conducted for the converted data is critically important, since it concerns with the reliability of the data. The check to validate whether or not the difference between the original data and the converted data is acceptable is “Equivalence Validation”. This part of ISO 10303 deals with this equivalence validation for product data.

It shall be noted that the comparison for checking equivalence is not conducted for arbitrary selected two product data, but conducted for the two product data where one is obtained from the other by data conversion. Therefore, there is an assumption that the two product data are not very different but are similar from various points of view.

There are various constituent data types of product data. In this edition of ISO 10303, shape data equivalence, assembly data equivalence, and PMI data equivalence are targets of equivalence validation. Equivalence of other constituents of product data will be dealt with in the future editions.

This part of ISO 10303 consists of five mutually related schemas as shown in Figure 1. The first schema, namely the product data equivalence definition schema, defines high level data elements for managing product data equivalence information such as the relationship of the product data equivalence information with the pertaining product data inspected. The second schema, namely the product data equivalence criteria schema, deals with general product data and specifies how to represent equivalence criteria and equivalence inspection requirements. The third schema, namely the product data equivalence inspection result schema, also deals with general product data and specifies how to represent equivalence inspection results. Specifications defined in the second and the third schemas are sufficiently general so that equivalence of various types of product data other than shape data, assembly data and PMI data can be dealt with in the future.

Two schemas, the annotated model 3d equivalence criteria schema and the annotated model 3d equivalence inspection result schema are specializations of the product data equivalence criteria schema and the product data equivalence inspection result schema respectively to annotated model 3d data. The equivalence of annotated model 3d data is further classified into Shape data equivalence, Assembly data equivalence, and PMI equivalence, that are standardized in detail in the annotated_model_3d_equivalence_shape_module, annotated_model_3d_equivalence_assembly module and annotated_model_3d_equivalence_PMI module respectively.

The target product shape data representation is limited in this part of ISO 10303 to the data conforming to ISO 10303-42, and some APs based on ISO 10303-42. The target assembly data representation also is limited to ISO 10303 relevant representation though the treatment of leaf component part shape representation is similar to that of ISO 10303-242. The target PMI data presentation and representation is similar to ISO 10303-242.

Each criterion for equivalence validation may include a pertaining inspection requirement. An inspection requirement provides a textual description of how the criterion is to be inspected and may have additional attributes and rules to control the test and the element or elements to be tested. It does not provide an algorithm for the inspection process. It is expected that the developer of a product data validation checking program will develop his algorithm for the inspection so that that algorithm satisfies the inspection requirement specified.

NOTE 1   Inspection algorithms are outside the scope of this part of ISO 10303 since it is understood that algorithm development is a competitive arena for engineering system vendors and cannot be standardized by an international standard.

For the assessment of equivalence validation by numerical test, thresholds which are user definable from application protocols supporting above described shape, assembly and PMI data representations, such as ISO 10303-242[1], play a key role. An example of a typical threshold is a distance threshold for validating the maximum deviation of the original and converted product data. That distance threshold implies that if the maximum distance between corresponding position of the two product data is greater than or equal to the specified minimum value, then the deviation shall be understood as unacceptable deviation. Appropriate thresholds depend on many factors such as size of a product, design requirements, sensitivity of engineering systems to numerical imprecision, etc. Therefore, what threshold value to use needs to be carefully determined in each business situation based on agreement among business partners.

The product data equivalence inspection result schema provides representation of equivalence inspection results for two specified product data with regard to specified equivalence criteria. The inspection result indicates whether or not the two product data inspected contain unacceptable difference. It may also include detailed information on what type of unacceptable difference is existing, and how serious the difference is, together with the product data element where the problem is detected. This information is expected to be useful for the equivalence improvement process.

Constituent schemas of this part of ISO 10303 are as follows:

  • Product_data_equivalence_definition schema: defines high level data elements for managing product data equivalence validation information;
  • Product_data_equivalence_criteria schema: provides general specifications for the representation of equivalence validation criteria, equivalence inspection requirements and equivalence assessment specifications for product data;
  • Product_data_equivalence_inspection_result schema: provides general specifications for the representation of equivalence inspection results for a particular couple of product data;
  • Annotate_mode_3d_equivalence_criteria schema: provides representations of equivalence validation criteria for annotated model 3d data. together with the pertaining inspection requirements, assessment specifications and thresholds for judging whether or not equivalence is satisfied;
  • Annotated_model_3d_inspection_result schema: provides representations of equivalence inspection results for a particular couple of product data with regard to specified equivalence criteria. Detailed information on what type of non-equivalence is existing, and how serious it is, together with the product element data where the problem is detected can also be represented.
