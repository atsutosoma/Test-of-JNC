5.2 Fundamental concepts and assumptions

In order to unambiguously share product data equivalence information, the following are required:

  1. A clear statement of each equivalence requirement. Such a requirement will be referred to as a ‘equivalence criterion’ in this standard;
  2. Specification of necessary conditions for the inspection to be used for ensuring that equivalence criteria are met. Such conditions are referred to as ‘inspection requirement’ in this standard. Each inspection requirement shall specify the types of entities to be checked, the inspection process to be used (if definable), and the required accuracy of the inspection. Inspection processes are specified in descriptive terms only — the actual inspection algorithms are outside the scope of this standard;
  3. Specification of criteria for judging the acceptability of the equivalence inspection. Such specifications are referred to as ‘assessment specifications’ in this standard. Assessment specifications for the equivalence of annotated model 3d fall into two classes, the first involving numerical tests with one or two thresholds for acceptability, and the second involving logical tests (details will be presented in clause 7);
  4. A method for the representation of the requirements for reporting on the equivalence of annotated model 3d;
  5. A method for the representation of inspection results;

This schema provides representations for items a. – d. in the foregoing list, which are mandatory in any application.

NOTE 1   Scenarios of the expected use of this standard are described in Annex G.

NOTE 2   The information corresponding to e. above is specified in product_data_equivalence_inspection_result_schema.

5.2.1 Representation of product data equivalence criteria

Product data equivalence can be unambiguously represented by a combination of equivalence criteria. Each criterion may have one or more associated inspection requirement and assessment specification.

It is anticipated that there are equivalence criteria of which inspection requirement is not possible to clearly specify. For this reason, this schema provides both equivalence criteria with or without relationship to pertaining inspection requirement.

EXAMPLE 1   ‘implied semantics shall be completely converted’ is a clear and important product data equivalence requirement. In this case, ‘identical implied semantics’ will become a criterion. Therefore, satisfaction of the criterion implies existence of equivalence defect. But, unambiguous specification of identical implied semantics will be quite difficult to define.

EXAMPLE 2   Significant deviation between corresponding positions of the two product data will be problematic for receiver side since it will deteriorate reliability of the data. In order to detect this type of non-equivalent data, the criterion ‘ domain_matching_of_arcwise-connected_surfaces’ as defined in the annotated_model_3d_equivalence_shape_module is prepared. In order to judge the equivalence, related inspection shall be based on the calculation of distance between two corresponding surfaces. The distance shall be calculated ideally for any point on a surface as minimum distance to the corresponding surface. After calculating this distance for all points on the surface, maximum value among them shall be obtained. Then this maximum distance shall be compared with the threshold defined for this criterion for evaluating if non-equivalent defect is existing or not. This example shows combination of quality criterion, associated inspection requirement and assessment specification form core part of equivalence validation model representation.

Equivalence requirement is application dependent and target design requirement dependent. This schema allows users to select a set of necessary criteria from the standardized criteria and combine them to represent his requirements on product data equivalence.

Specifications in this schema are enough general so that they can be applied for equivalence of any property of a product data. Specialization of this schema to annotated model 3d data is given in clause 7. Specialization to product data equivalence of any property other than annotated model 3d data will also be possible in the future as a specialization of this schema.

5.2.2 Correspondence of data elements to compare

Correspondence of data elements to compare, one in the original product model, the other in the converted model shall be exactly known. As for shape model, external specification of correspondence finding algorithm is given in the annotated_model_3d_shape_equivalence module.

As for assembly model and PMI data, it is understood that there is no commonly used method for detecting the correspondence, but the use of user given name when applicable, or bounding box for knowing spatial extent, etc. are used. In the definition of criteria in annotated_model_3d_equivalence_assembly module and annotated_model_3d_equivalence_PMI module, it is assumed that the correspondence is known outside of those standards. If unique naming method such as UUID is developed in the future, it is assumed that unique name will be transferred via STEP translator.

5.2.3 Representation of the requirement on inspection of product data quivalence

In order to inspect if a non-equivalent defect is existing or not, reliable inspection shall be associated to each equivalence criterion.

The following are requirements for reliable inspection:

  1. Appropriate data element shall be inspected.
  2. Necessary conditions for appropriate inspection shall be clarified such as mandatory inspection process if definable, what physical quantity shall be inspected, what special cases shall be taken into account, required precision of inspection result, etc.
  3. Robust algorithm that satisfies all the requirements represented by 1. above shall be deployed.

It is understood that inspection algorithm belongs to competitive arena for engineering system vendors and therefore shall not be standardized by an International Standard. By this reason, requirement 3. above is outside the scope of this standard.

The representation of inspection requirement is designed so that it can be used both for equivalence criteria representation and inspection result representation.

5.2.4 Control of inspection result report

Type of information and level of details of inspection result report may differ depending on the scenario of its use or the purpose of the inspection. The entity data_equivalence_report_request provides the representation of a request for an inspection report with regard to a particular criterion. It has two subtypes, summary_equivalence_report_request and detailed_equivalence_report_request. The use of summary_equivalence_report_request is recommended to capture overall equivalence level such as how many entities were checked regarding the criterion and how many entities among them were judged as having non-equivalent defect. The use of detailed_equivalence_report_request is suited to obtain more detailed report such as what entity instance caused non-equivalent defect and how serious it is. It has attributes to request a report on all the inspected elements or only on elements with non-equivalent defect. Expected number of elements to be reported and expected sequence of report may also be specified. Since these specifications affects to other schemas in this standard, it is recommended to look into Figure 1 of Introduction to understand related entities in other schemas. data_equivalence_inspection_report of product_data_equivalence_inspection_result_schema corresponds to data_equivalence_report_request of this schema. It has two subtypes, data_equivalence_inspection_criterion_report which corresponds to summary_equivalence_report_request of this schema and data_equivalence_inspection_instance_report which corresponds to detailed_equivalence_report_request of this schema. data_equivalence_inspection_instance_report is a collection of data_equivalence_inspection_instance_report_item which corresponds to one instance or one pair of instances of the given product data.

NOTE    In annotated_model_3d_equivalence_inspection_result_schema, data_equivalence_inspection_inspection_instance_report_item is specialized to annotated_model_3d_equivalence_inspection_instance_report_item which has a subtype instance_report_item_with_extreme_instances for representing non-equivalent defect in annotated_model_3d instance level and how serious it is.

EXAMPLE    The following explanation assumes the case where only report on non-equivalent elements is requested. In the case of ‘ domain matching by Gaussian G2 continuity’ criterion as defined in shape_equivalence_module, two or more surfaces may be detected as having non-equivalent defect by losing G2 continuity. The entity data_equivalence_inspection_instance_report (or annotated_model_3d_equivalence_inspection_instance_report) will report all the detected surfaces. The entity data_equivalence_inspection_instance_report_item (or annotated_model_3d_equivalence_inspection_instance_report_item) corresponds to one detected surface.
